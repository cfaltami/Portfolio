Biography

My name is Felipe Altamirano, and I am a computer and electrical engineering student at Michigan Technological University. I was born and raised in Malargüe, Argentina, where my interest in technology began at a young age—I used to disassemble clocks, computers, and whatever else I could find. What began as early curiosity became a distinct aim at the age of seven, when a talk in an English class opened my eyes to the prospect of studying abroad. From that point forward, I dedicated myself to my dream, studying hard in school to achieve high grades and improve my talents. In 2023, after more than a decade of work, I was offered a full scholarship to study in the United States.

×

At MTU, I've continued to develop both intellectually and emotionally. I am an IT technician on campus, where I fix hardware and software issues and maintain network infrastructure. I'm also involved in student organizations such as Alpha Phi Omega, where I've held leadership positions and coordinated service initiatives. My technological interests include embedded systems, computer hardware, and programming with languages such as HTML, Java, JavaScript, C++, Python, assembly code and Bash. I've always seen engineering as a method to bring ideas to life via logic and creativity, and I'm looking forward to continuing to develop, learn, and contribute in and outside the classroom.
